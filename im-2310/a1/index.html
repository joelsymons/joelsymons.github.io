    Given that the page loaded up with a blank canvas, similar to a basic Paint style program, my attention went straight to the buttons along the left side and along the top to see what actions I could take with the canvas.

  3. What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

    The paintbrush tool and drawing on the canvas

  4. What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

    Clicking and dragging

    To provide an extremely simple canvas that a client can use to draw lines or shapes with certain patterns

  7. What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

    I think that because the experience is designed for easy and accessible use (and given the nature of it being a blank canvas) it is intended to be interacted with as much or as little as the user desires.

  8. What is your impression of how the interactive experience communicates how it should be interacted with over time?

    The experiences doesn't explicitly describe how it should be interacted with over time, but the one way it does communicate this is with the "File" button in the top left corner, which lets you not only save the image you've created but also import images into the website and it will automatically render them using the program's own patterns and paintbrush styles.
